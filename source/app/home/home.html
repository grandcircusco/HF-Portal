WHAT IS HACKER FELLOWS?

Hacker Fellows is a one-year fellowship program where recent Computer Science college graduates work as a developer at a Michigan start-up. Hacker Fellows start with 5 weeks of cohort-based training before diving into Michigan start-up. Hacker Fellows are an elite group of developers passionate about making an impact through tech. Cool, huh?

BOOTCAMP

An elite group of selected fellows will train in the Grand Circus classroom space in downtown Detroit over the course of five weeks. During this time, Hacker Fellows will acquire the entrepreneurial and technical skills needed to work for an amazing start-up in Michigan. Fellows will also develop a strong cohort of peers and start-up junkies. By being immersed in the start-up ecosystem, Fellows will create relationships and develop a firm understanding of the business side of start-ups.

WORKING AT A STARTUP

Fellows will be thrown into the deep end and given the opportunity to make a huge impact on the high-growth high-demand tech sector. Fellows will be matched with a Michigan start-up following training and work as a developer for the remainder of the year-long fellowship. Hacker Fellows will also participate in ongoing mentorship and activities throughout the year. This program will provide the support required to create a "soft-landing" for recent Computer Science graduates coming to new cities in Michigan.

PAYOFF

Hacker Fellows addresses the #1 problem tech start-ups in Michigan report: finding top developer talent for companies. The primary goals of Hacker Fellows are:

  • Attract tech entrepreneurs to Michigan start-ups
  • Promote job creation within the tech community
  • Fill the void of developers among Michigan start-ups and tech communities
  • Create a platform for mentoring, relationships and collaboration within the tech community
  • Boost Hacker Fellows compensation by ~$20,000 to help attract the top talent

REQUIREMENTS

  • Backed by a venture capital company or angel investor

  • Have substantial operations in Michigan

  • Early stage start-up (less than 5 years old)

  • For profit business

  • Doing cool techy things, commercializing innovative technology

  • Willing to commit to hiring and keeping a computer science major over the one-year fellowship

OUR TEAM

INVEST DETROIT

Invest Detroit is deeply engaged in the entrepreneurial ecosystem throughout Michigan, particularly through two of its funds, the First Step Fund (a development fund that primarily invests at the pre-seed and seed stages) and Detroit Innovate (a venture fund that primarily invests at the seed and Series A stages). Combined, these two funds have 70 portfolio companies across the state but primarily in southeast Michigan.

The economic development efforts of Invest Detroit have resulted in 2,000 jobs created or retained through the successful implementation of entrepreneurial-based programs, investment funds, and services. Actively involving Michigan higher education institutions along with local start-ups will help to strengthen the tech community while also filling much needed skill set voids.

GRAND CIRCUS

Grand Circus is a training institute in the heart of downtown Detroit on a mission to elevate the tech community. We offer training, co-working and event hosting -- all under the same tent.

When it comes to training we dismiss that true skill comes with a certificate. We focus instead on outcomes that matter. With project based instruction our training delivers real world expertise. We call it training with a purpose.

Our curriculum is built on the latest in technology, business and design and we have partnered with the best and brightest. Our instructors are real practitioners who are at the top of their fields and committed to the success of their students.

We are located in the heart of Detroit, and the community is as central to our success as our location. Our space is available for co-working and events, because where there are relationships there are possibilities for learning.

FAQ's

1. How can I apply?
Currently the application window for our 2017 fellowship is open and you can apply at the top of this page. The application process includes an online application, phone interview, and in-person (or Google Hangout) interview. What are you waiting for?

2. What start-up will a fellow work at?
The Hacker Fellows portal will allow you to research the participating start-ups and schedule interviews. Fellows will work with the start-up that shares a common interest. Ultimately, it's up to you and the participating companies to decide where you work. The Hacker Fellows is actively involved in the recruiting process, but ultimately both the fellow and the company have to agree that there's a good fit!

3. Do you accept students with majors other than Computer Science?
This program accepts Computer Science majors and equivalent majors. (e.g. Computer Engineering, Computer Software, etc.). If you're a Hacker, you belong.

4. Will you sponsor work visas?
We welcome international fellows, but we cannot offer work sponsorship.
